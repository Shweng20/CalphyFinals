Characteristics Of Sound

Loudness

Loudness is measured in decibels (dB).

Loudness refers to how strong or intense a sound is perceived by the ear. It mainly depends on the amplitude and intensity of the sound wave ‚Äî the greater the amplitude, the louder the sound.

Characteristics Of Sound

Intensity is the amount of sound energy passing through an area in a given time. It shows how powerful a sound is and depends on the wave's amplitude ‚Äî larger amplitude means greater intensity. It is measured in watts per square meter (W/m¬≤).

Pitch
It allows us to distinguish musical notes and tones.
Pitch refers to how "high" or "low" a sound is perceived by the ear. It depends on the frequency of vibration ‚Äî higher frequency produces a higher pitch, while lower frequency results in a lower pitch.
Terms related to pitch:.
The fundamental frequency is the lowest frequency of vibration and determines the basic pitch of a sound. Overtones are the higher frequencies that accompany the fundamental, while harmonics are specific overtones that are whole-number multiples of the fundamental frequency.
Quality or timbre is the property that lets us tell sounds apart even if they have the same pitch and loudness. It depends on the mix of fundamental and harmonic frequencies, giving each sound its unique tone.
Quality or Timbre
It is also known as the tone color.
