SOUNDS

Sound is a mechanical longitudinal wave produced by the vibration of an object. When an object vibrates, it causes the surrounding medium (like air, water, or solids) to vibrate as well, creating a pattern of compressions (high-pressure regions) and rarefactions (low-pressure regions) that move outward from the source.

Sound requires a medium to travel.

  • Solid
  • Liquid
  • Gas

A sound propagates in a medium because there are particles that transmit the vibration.

The speed, pitch, and loudness of sound depend on the properties of the medium and the source of vibration.
